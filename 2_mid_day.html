Project 2 - "Mid Day Meal"

Government had launched MDM scheme to provide cooked noon meal to boost 'universalisation of primary education' in order to increase the enrolment, retention and attendance and simultaneously impacting on nutrition of students.

In this scheme, government provides construction cost for kitchen shades, free supply of food grains, labor cost & other administrative charges. It also provides the cooking cost (includes cooking ingredients like fuel, condiments, oil etc)

Stage Cooking cost per child per day (2014-15)
Primary 3.59
Upper Primary 5.38

This cooking cost is really less to manage the fuel required so the schools have to depend upon the stove/chulha for cooking the food.

However cooking on stove / chulha has its own disadvantages. In addition to causing pollution, one has to face the health hazards for eyes and throat. Food doesn't cook well and it also becomes difficult to light the chulha manually in rainy season.

So now, Fleetguard is supporting this scheme by providing initial gas set up - ( shegadi (gas stove) & cylinder) in schools in and around Nandur factory.

We surveyed 10 schools near Mirawadi & Sahajpur and analyzed the requirement. Here we involved our safety officer and a person from gas agency to prepare the safety checklist to ensure all the safety norms. After the survey, we finalized 7 schools for this initiative. 3 schools were rejected due to violation of safety norms.

After finalizing 7 schools we decided to do a pilot run in one school which was an Anganwadi (a pre-primary), at memanewadi for a month so that we could closely observe the safety norms being followed. Below you can see before & after photos. We have trained the school teacher and the cook on safety practices of using LPG gas & shegadi. The pilot run has successfully completed and now we have started providing the gas connections to other schools after the assurance of safety norms.
